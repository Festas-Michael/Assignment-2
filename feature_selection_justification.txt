  UNSINKABLE — TITANIC SURVIVAL ANALYSIS
  Feature Selection Justification Report
  Part 3: Feature Selection (10 Marks)

METHOD SUMMARY
──────────────
Two-step selection process was used:

  Step 1 — Correlation Filter (threshold: |r| > 0.90)
           Removed features that were near-perfect mirrors of another feature.
           Kept the one with the stronger link to survival.

  Step 2 — Random Forest Importance Ranking
           Trained a Random Forest (300 trees) on remaining features.
           Ranked every feature by how much it contributed to correct predictions.
           Mean importance score = 0.0286 (used as the cutoff threshold).
           Features scoring ABOVE this threshold → KEPT.
           Features scoring BELOW this threshold → DROPPED.

Mean importance threshold: 0.0286
Total features before selection: 35
Total features selected: 12
Total features dropped: 23

================================================================================
  SECTION A — FEATURES KEPT (12 features)
================================================================================

These 12 features all scored above the mean importance threshold of 0.0286
in the Random Forest ranking. Each one carries a meaningful, unique signal
that helps the model predict who survived.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Sex_male  |  Importance: 0.1760  |  Rank: 1st out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  The single strongest predictor in the entire dataset. Female passengers
  survived at 74.8% while male passengers survived at only 22.3% — a gap
  of over 50 percentage points. This reflects the "women and children first"
  lifeboat boarding rule enforced by the Titanic's crew.

  Sex_female was DROPPED during the correlation filter (Step 1) because it is
  the exact mathematical mirror of Sex_male (|r| = 1.0). Keeping both would
  add zero new information. Sex_male was chosen over Sex_female because both
  carry identical predictive power — only one is needed.

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  Being male was the biggest single disadvantage for survival on the Titanic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. IsWoman_or_Child  |  Importance: 0.1137  |  Rank: 2nd out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  This is an engineered feature (created in Part 2) that combines Sex and
  AgeGroup into one binary signal: 1 = female OR under 13 years old, 0 = else.
  It directly encodes the "women and children first" evacuation rule as a
  machine-readable variable. Its importance score of 0.1137 confirms the rule
  was genuinely followed — making it the 2nd most powerful predictor.

  Although it overlaps with Sex_male, it adds extra signal by capturing
  young boys (Masters) who also received priority — something Sex_male alone
  misses.

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  Being a woman OR a child gave you the strongest chance of getting a
  lifeboat seat. This feature captures that rule directly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. Title_Mr  |  Importance: 0.0974  |  Rank: 3rd out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  Title was extracted from the passenger Name column (e.g. "Smith, Mr. John"
  → "Mr"). Title_Mr flags adult men specifically — a group with the lowest
  survival rate on the ship. It adds signal beyond Sex_male because it also
  excludes male children (who have "Master" as their title and had much higher
  survival rates). This distinction between adult men and male children is
  something Sex_male alone cannot capture.

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  "Mr" meant adult male — the group least likely to survive. The title
  column separated adult men from boys in a way that raw sex alone could not.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. Age_x_Pclass  |  Importance: 0.0773  |  Rank: 4th out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  This is an interaction feature (Age multiplied by passenger class). It
  captures a compound effect: being old AND in a lower class was significantly
  more dangerous than either factor alone. A 60-year-old in 3rd class faced
  compounding disadvantages — reduced mobility AND a cabin far from lifeboats.
  A 60-year-old in 1st class had crew assistance and closer lifeboat access.
  The interaction term captures this nuance that neither Age nor Pclass
  could express separately.

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  Age penalised you more if you were also in a lower class. Multiplying
  the two numbers together captures that double disadvantage in one feature.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5. LogFare  |  Importance: 0.0602  |  Rank: 5th out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  LogFare is the log-transformed version of the ticket price (log1p(Fare)).
  The raw Fare column has extreme right skew — most passengers paid under
  £30 but a handful paid over £200, which distorts the model. Applying a
  log transform compresses that skew and makes the distribution roughly
  normal. LogFare outranked raw Fare slightly (0.0602 vs 0.0572) but both
  were kept because they capture slightly different aspects of the price
  signal — the transformed shape vs the raw magnitude.

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  Ticket price strongly predicted survival (richer = better odds). The log
  version makes that price signal cleaner for the model to learn from.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. Fare  |  Importance: 0.0572  |  Rank: 6th out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  Raw ticket price. Wealthier passengers had access to upper-deck cabins
  closer to lifeboats, and were more likely to be in 1st class where survival
  rates were highest. Despite being skewed, the raw Fare value still scored
  above the mean importance threshold. Kept alongside LogFare because the
  two are not perfectly correlated (|r| = 0.87, just below the 0.90 removal
  threshold) and each captures a slightly different signal.

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  Simply put — the more you paid for your ticket, the better your survival
  odds. This raw price figure still adds useful information to the model.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7. LogAge  |  Importance: 0.0529  |  Rank: 7th out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  Log-transformed version of Age (log1p(Age)). Age has a mild right skew
  and several outliers among elderly passengers. The log transform smooths
  this and helps distance-based models (like KNN or SVM) that are sensitive
  to scale. LogAge and Age are both kept because they were not perfectly
  correlated after transformation and each scored above the threshold.

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  Age affected survival — children had priority, elderly passengers struggled
  to reach lifeboats. The log version gives the model a cleaner version of
  that age signal.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
8. Age  |  Importance: 0.0518  |  Rank: 8th out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  Raw passenger age. Children under 13 had priority boarding. Elderly
  passengers (60+) had reduced mobility and lower survival rates. Age
  scored just above the importance threshold (0.0518 vs threshold 0.0286)
  and was retained. It adds complementary signal to LogAge — the raw
  magnitude of age (e.g. "this person is 65") vs the transformed shape.

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  How old you were mattered. Very young passengers got priority; very old
  passengers were disadvantaged. The model needs this raw number.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9. FarePerPerson  |  Importance: 0.0518  |  Rank: 9th out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  This is an engineered feature: Fare divided by FamilySize. It normalises
  ticket price by group size. A family of 4 paying £40 total spent £10 per
  person — very different from a solo traveller paying £40. FarePerPerson
  is a better proxy for individual wealth than raw Fare. It scored above the
  threshold and is not highly correlated with Fare alone (a family's total
  fare and their per-person fare can differ substantially).

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  Splitting the ticket price by group size gives a fairer measure of how
  wealthy each individual passenger actually was.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
10. Pclass_x_Fare  |  Importance: 0.0484  |  Rank: 10th out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  Passenger class multiplied by ticket price. This interaction captures the
  combined wealth-and-status signal: a 1st-class passenger who also paid a
  high fare had compounding survival advantages (crew priority + upper deck
  proximity + social status). A 3rd-class passenger who paid little had
  compounding disadvantages. Neither Pclass nor Fare alone captures this
  compound effect as effectively as their product.

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  Being in a higher class AND paying more created compounding advantages.
  Multiplying the two together captures that combined effect.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11. Title_Miss  |  Importance: 0.0453  |  Rank: 11th out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  "Miss" was the title given to unmarried women and girls. This group had
  high survival rates reflecting both lifeboat priority (female) and
  typically younger age. Title_Miss adds signal beyond Sex_male because it
  specifically identifies young/unmarried females — a sub-group that
  survived at particularly high rates compared to married women (Mrs) or
  elderly women. The distinction between Miss and Mrs carried real
  demographic meaning aboard the Titanic.

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  Unmarried women and girls survived at very high rates. This title flag
  captures that specific group more precisely than sex alone.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12. Title_Mrs  |  Importance: 0.0445  |  Rank: 12th out of 35
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY KEPT:
  "Mrs" identified married women. Like Title_Miss, this group received
  lifeboat priority. However, married women were often travelling with
  families — some stayed behind with husbands or struggled to board with
  children — leading to slightly different survival patterns than Miss.
  Title_Mrs scored just above the threshold and provides a meaningful
  demographic distinction that complements Title_Miss.

PLAIN ENGLISH:
  Married women also had high survival rates but with slightly different
  patterns to unmarried women. The model benefits from knowing the difference.

================================================================================
  SECTION B — FEATURES DROPPED (23 features)
================================================================================

These features were removed either in the correlation filter (Step 1) or
because they scored BELOW the mean importance threshold of 0.0286 in the
Random Forest ranking (Step 2). Each one added too little new information
to justify including it in the final model.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
13. Pclass  |  Importance: 0.0255  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Passenger class (1st, 2nd, 3rd) on its own scored below the threshold
  once the interaction features Pclass_x_Fare and Age_x_Pclass were
  introduced. Those two interaction terms absorbed and surpassed the raw
  class signal. Keeping Pclass alongside its own interactions would be
  partially redundant.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
14. Title_Rare_Male  |  Importance: 0.0119  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Rare male titles (Dr, Rev, Col, Capt, Major) collectively represent a
  very small number of passengers. Their survival patterns were inconsistent
  — some high-status males survived, others did not — making this a noisy,
  low-information feature. Scored well below threshold.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
15. FamilySize  |  Importance: 0.0115  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  FamilySize was used to create FarePerPerson and IsAlone — those derived
  features absorbed its predictive signal more effectively. The raw
  FamilySize number itself scored below threshold once those derivatives
  were present. Redundant in the presence of its own children.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
16. SibSp  |  Importance: 0.0109  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Number of siblings/spouses aboard. Once FamilySize was engineered
  (SibSp + Parch + 1), the raw SibSp column became redundant — FamilySize
  already captures this information in a more complete form. Low importance
  score confirms this.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
17. Parch  |  Importance: 0.0090  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Number of parents/children aboard. Same reasoning as SibSp — once
  FamilySize was created, the individual components SibSp and Parch became
  largely redundant. Scored well below threshold.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
18. Age_was_missing  |  Importance: 0.0067  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  This indicator flag (1 = Age was originally missing, 0 = Age was present)
  was created during data cleaning to preserve the fact that some ages were
  imputed. However, it scored very low — meaning whether or not a passenger's
  age was missing did not meaningfully affect survival prediction beyond what
  the imputed Age value itself already captured.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
19. IsAlone  |  Importance: 0.0065  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Binary flag for passengers travelling alone. While solo travellers did
  have lower survival rates, this signal was already captured more
  effectively by FarePerPerson (solo travellers have higher per-person fares)
  and FamilySize interactions. IsAlone on its own added minimal additional
  information after those features were present.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
20. Embarked_S  |  Importance: 0.0064  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Flag for passengers who boarded at Southampton. Port of embarkation showed
  some correlation with class distribution (Southampton had more 3rd-class
  passengers) but this class information was already captured more directly
  by Pclass and Fare features. Embarked features added little new signal.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
21. Cabin_known  |  Importance: 0.0058  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Binary flag for whether a cabin number was recorded (1 = yes, 0 = no).
  While cabin availability correlated with class (1st-class passengers were
  more likely to have recorded cabins), this signal was already better
  represented by Fare, Pclass, and the Deck features. Low importance
  confirmed it added minimal unique information.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
22. Title_Rare_Female  |  Importance: 0.0050  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Rare female titles (Lady, Countess, Dona) represent an extremely small
  number of passengers — too few for the model to learn a reliable pattern
  from. Their survival signal was already captured by Sex_male and
  IsWoman_or_Child. Negligible importance score.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
23. AgeGroup_Child  |  Importance: 0.0049  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Binary flag for passengers under 13. The child survival signal was already
  captured more powerfully by IsWoman_or_Child (which explicitly includes
  children) and Age/LogAge (which capture the continuous age relationship).
  The separate AgeGroup_Child flag added redundant information.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
24. AgeGroup_Adult  |  Importance: 0.0040  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Adults (age 20-59) are the majority of passengers. This flag was
  essentially the complement of Child + Teen + Senior flags — it added
  little discriminating power since most passengers fell into this group.
  Scored well below threshold.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
25. Embarked_C  |  Importance: 0.0039  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Flag for passengers who boarded at Cherbourg. Cherbourg passengers were
  slightly wealthier on average but this wealth signal was already captured
  by Fare and Pclass. The port itself added minimal predictive value
  independently. Below threshold.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
26. Embarked_Q  |  Importance: 0.0024  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Flag for passengers who boarded at Queenstown. This port was predominantly
  3rd-class Irish emigrants — however, that class signal was already captured
  by Pclass and Fare far more directly. Scored near zero importance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
27. Deck_D  |  Importance: 0.0023  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Individual deck flags (A through G) all scored very low. While deck
  location correlated with class, that class signal was already captured
  by Fare, Pclass, and their interaction. Additionally, 79% of passengers
  had no cabin recorded — making all Deck flags based on a small, potentially
  unrepresentative subset of passengers.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
28-34. Deck_A, Deck_B, Deck_C, Deck_E, Deck_F, Deck_G  |  DROPPED
        Importances: 0.0013, 0.0003, 0.0003, 0.0010, 0.0006, 0.0014
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  All individual deck letter flags scored extremely close to zero. The same
  reasoning as Deck_D applies. With 79.1% of cabin values missing, these
  features describe only ~186 passengers out of 891 — too small a subset
  to produce reliable survival patterns. The deck information that does
  exist is already proxied by Fare and Pclass.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
35. AgeGroup_Teen  |  Importance: 0.0022  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Teenagers (13-19) showed no distinct survival pattern separate from adults.
  They were old enough to not receive the "children first" priority but young
  enough to not face the mobility disadvantages of seniors. The continuous
  Age and LogAge features already capture this age range without needing a
  separate flag.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
36. AgeGroup_Senior  |  Importance: 0.0000  |  DROPPED — below threshold
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY DROPPED:
  Passengers over 60. Scored essentially zero importance — the lowest of all
  35 features. The senior survival disadvantage was already captured by the
  continuous Age and LogAge features, and by the Age_x_Pclass interaction.
  A separate binary Senior flag added nothing the model could not already
  learn from the age value itself.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
NOTE — Features removed in Correlation Filter (Step 1, before RF ranking):
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Sex_female   → Perfect mirror of Sex_male (|r| = 1.0). Identical
                 information. Sex_male was retained as it was encountered
                 first in the ranking. Keeping both would be pure redundancy.

  Deck_U       → "Unknown deck" flag was highly correlated with Cabin_known
                 (|r| > 0.90) — they describe the same thing from opposite
                 directions. Cabin_known was retained as it had marginally
                 higher correlation with survival.

  FINAL SUMMARY
================================================================================

  Features KEPT (12):
  ───────────────────
  Sex_male, IsWoman_or_Child, Title_Mr, Age_x_Pclass, LogFare,
  Fare, LogAge, Age, FarePerPerson, Pclass_x_Fare, Title_Miss, Title_Mrs

  Features DROPPED (23):
  ──────────────────────
  Pclass, Title_Rare_Male, FamilySize, SibSp, Parch, Age_was_missing,
  IsAlone, Embarked_S, Cabin_known, Title_Rare_Female, AgeGroup_Child,
  AgeGroup_Adult, Embarked_C, Embarked_Q, Deck_D, Deck_A, Deck_B,
  Deck_C, Deck_E, Deck_F, Deck_G, AgeGroup_Teen, AgeGroup_Senior
  + Sex_female and Deck_U (removed in correlation filter)

  Key insight:
  ────────────
  The 12 selected features reduce noise while preserving the five most
  important survival signals identified in this dataset:

    1. Gender (Sex_male, IsWoman_or_Child, Title_Mr, Title_Miss, Title_Mrs)
    2. Wealth  (Fare, LogFare, FarePerPerson, Pclass_x_Fare)
    3. Age     (Age, LogAge, Age_x_Pclass)
    4. Social rank  (Title features proxy class + gender simultaneously)
    5. Compound effects  (interaction terms capture what single features miss)

  5-fold Cross-Validated ROC-AUC with selected features: 0.787
  (A score of 1.0 = perfect prediction. 0.5 = random guessing.)
